# Artificial intelligence to hit workplace like a freight train, energy boss warns

Sky News reported comments from Greg Jackson, founder of Octopus Energy, about the company's use of generative AI in customer service operations.

Key reported points:

- Octopus saw benefits from generative AI in customer service.
- **44% of customer emails** were being answered, at least in part, by AI **seven weeks after rollout**.
- Human employees still managed and checked the AI's output.
- Jackson said the AI deployment would not cost jobs at Octopus.
- Jackson framed AI as enabling growing companies to expand faster and serve customers better, while warning that companies focused on cost-cutting could use it as a job-replacement tool.

Sky News also reported a comparison case from Allen & Overy, where a bespoke AI tool was used by 3,500 lawyers and delivered one or two hours per week of productivity gain per person.

## Related reported metric from City A.M.

City A.M. separately reported that Jackson had said the technology was doing the work of **250 people** answering customer emails and had received **80% customer satisfaction**, compared with **65%** achieved by workers.
