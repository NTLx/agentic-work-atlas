# The AI Jobs Transition Framework for the EU

**Author:** Alex Martin Richmond
**Date:** June 2026
**Organization:** OpenAI Economic Research
**Sign-off:** Ronnie Chatterji, OpenAI Chief Economist

---

## 中文摘要

OpenAI 经济研究部发布 AI 就业转型框架的欧盟版本，将 2609 个 ESCO 职业按三条维度（技术暴露度 + 人类必要性 + 需求弹性）映射为四条转型路径：

| 路径 | 欧盟占比 | 美国占比 | 含义 |
|------|---------|---------|------|
| 更高自动化潜力 | **14%** | 18% | AI 可直接替代大部分任务 |
| 将重组 | **27%** | 24% | AI 改变工作流但不消灭岗位 |
| 可能随 AI 增长 | **12%** | 12% | 成本下降扩大需求 |
| 短期变化较小 | **47%** | 46% | 物理/监管/关系壁垒限制替代 |

**欧盟特色**：比美国更小的自动化份额、更大的重组份额，反映欧洲制造业/护理/教育/公共服务的职业结构差异。

**人类必要性分类**（为什么人仍然不可替代）：49% 物理性、28% 监管/问责、9% 关系性、14% 无明确障碍。

**核心洞见**：技术能力本身不决定劳动力市场结果——制度（许可、公共预算、法定服务义务、多语言要求、人际信任）决定了技术能力如何转化为实际就业变化。

### From the Desk of the Chief Economist

Europe is entering a new phase of the AI transition. Across workplaces, households, firms, and public institutions, AI is beginning to change how people access information, make decisions, and get work done...

Key takeaways:
1. EU: 12% growth, 14% higher automation, 27% reorganize, 47% less immediate change
2. Country variation: Luxembourg/Sweden/Netherlands have largest growth shares; Germany/Greece/Italy have largest automation shares
3. Demand elasticity: lower costs may increase demand where work is digitally deliverable/discretionary/project-based
4. Human involvement: 49% physical, 28% regulatory/accountability, 9% relational, 14% residual

### Framework: Three questions
- Technical exposure: how extensively can AI perform the occupation's tasks?
- Human necessity: do regulation, accountability, physical presence, or relationships keep a worker at the center?
- Demand elasticity: do lower costs expand quantity enough to offset labor-saving gains?

### Policy implications
- AI Observatory: live labor-market intelligence connecting occupation, skills, vacancy, wage, training data
- National readiness plans linking AI adoption, skills, public employment services, social dialogue
- Universal AI literacy
- Portable skills, work-compatible training, career guidance

### Methodology
- ESCO-O*NET crosswalk (4253 links across 2609 ESCO occupations)
- Europe-tailored human necessity and demand elasticity estimates
- Model-based estimates, not observed causal data
- Country differences reflect occupational composition, not readiness ranking
