## 中文摘要

咨询巨头 Accenture 正试图阻止非技术员工在 trivial 任务上烧光公司的 AI token 预算——例如把 PDF 转成 PPT。根据 404 Media 获得的泄露内部音频，Accenture 的 agentic AI 策略负责人 Justice Kwak 表示，内部数据显示**驱动 token 消耗的其实不是工程师，而是大量非工程师**在执行这些行为。

这一现象标志着 AI 无节制增长时代的终结：
- **GitHub Copilot** 已从固定订阅转为按 token 计费
- **Uber** CTO 透露四个月烧完全年 AI 预算，随后限制 Claude Code 和 Cursor 使用
- **Accenture** 要求高级员工使用 AI，否则影响晋升

这也削弱了"超级工程师生成海量代码驱动 AI 繁荣"的主流叙事——在很多情况下，是非技术人员在非专业任务上烧 token。

## 原文 (Preview)

Consulting giant Accenture is trying to figure out how to stop non-technical workers from blowing through companies' AI token budget on trivial tasks like converting PDFs to presentation slides, according to leaked audio obtained by 404 Media. Across the industry Accenture is seeing "soaring token spend," according to the audio.

The news highlights a major shift in the tech industry and other companies that use AI: the wave of uninhibited AI growth is over. Some AI providers like GitHub are now [charging customers per token](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/30/what-a-joke-github-copilots-new-token-based-billing-spurs-consternation-among-devs/?ref=404media.co) rather than a flat subscription fee, leading some companies to burn through their tokens. Uber recently [capped employees' use of AI tools](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-02/uber-caps-usage-of-ai-tools-like-claude-code-to-cut-costs?utm_source=website&utm_medium=share&utm_campaign=twitter&embedded-checkout=true) like Claude Code and Cursor; that came after Uber told employees to use AI as much as possible and Uber's CTO said the company had [blown its entire AI budget](https://www.theinformation.com/newsletters/applied-ai/uber-cto-shows-claude-code-can-blow-ai-budgets?im_ref=zELUMKyRUxycRDfQPrT4BXOzUkuT9UW3mwKY000&sharedid=techcrunch.com&irpid=10078&utm_term=techcrunch.com&irgwc=1&afsrc=1&utm_source=affiliate&utm_medium=cpa&utm_campaign=10078-Skimbit+Ltd.) in four months. And Accenture itself reportedly started requiring [senior staff to start using AI](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/19/accenture-ai-orders-senior-staff-lose-out-promotions.html?ref=404media.co) or risk losing out on promotions.

It also undercuts the narrative that superpowered engineers generating mountains of code are behind the AI boom. In many cases it is non-technical staff burning through tokens for non-specialized tasks.

"We're seeing from some of the data internally at least that it's actually not our engineers that are driving the token consumption. It's a lot of the non-engineers that are doing some of those behaviors [...] you were talking about," Justice Kwak, Accenture's agentic AI strategy lead, said in a recent internal meeting, according to the audio obtained by 404 Media.
